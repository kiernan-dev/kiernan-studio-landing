Every major failure I've had in this career taught me something essential. The jQuery application that couldn't scale taught me about architecture. I've seen companies chase shiny new frameworks while their users struggled with basic functionality. I've been in meetings where everyone argued about architecture while ignoring the fact that the app took 10 seconds to load. It only matters if it makes the end product genuinely better, not just more impressive on paper.
